Course Outline

The course examines the fundamental theories and updated research progress in computational linguistics. It will cover the goals and methods of computational linguistics in historical perspective, and then delves in some detail into the essential concepts of linguistic structure and analysis, interpretation, and language use, as well as acquisition of knowledge for language, statistical and machine learning techniques in natural language processing, and miscellaneous applications.

Foundations

Linguistic structures that make language work

  • Historical goals and methods of CL
  • Phonetics, phonology, and morphology
  • Syntax and parsing techniques
  • Semantics and meaning representation
  • Pragmatics and discourse analysis

Techniques

Computational methods for processing language

  • Statistical methods for language
  • Machine learning for NLP
  • Deep learning and neural networks
  • Language modeling basics
  • Transformers and pre-trained models

Applications

Real-world uses and research practice

  • Knowledge acquisition and representation
  • Language interpretation
  • Language use in real-world contexts
  • Multilingual and cross-lingual NLP
  • Applications in society and industry

Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Understand key concepts related to computational linguistics and language-based artificial intelligence.
  2. Apply computational approaches to text and language in basic analytical and project-based contexts.
  3. Reflect on the broader social and societal implications of language technologies.

Class Participation

Paper Sharing (20%)

Summarize and present a relevant research paper selected from instructor recommendations. Develops critical thinking and presentation skills.

Lab Engagement (20%)

Active participation in in-class lab activities and tasks, gaining hands-on experience in computational linguistics.

Midterm Project (30%)

Select a paper from class recommendations, thoroughly read it, explore citing papers, and identify a research stream for a literature review. Submit a written report and give a 15-minute presentation (5 min Q&A). Students may work individually or in teams.

Final Project (30%)

Build on the midterm proposal and make progress following the plan of action. Submit a final report documenting research process, findings, and conclusions. Give a final in-class presentation clearly explaining the research question, methods, results, and contributions. Team members must document individual contributions.

Late Policy

Under normal circumstances, coursework assignments will be marked within two weeks. If an assignment is submitted after the due date without prior permission from the instructor, it will receive a grade of zero. The above grade descriptor is subject to change.

Academic Honesty

Attention is drawn to University policy and regulations on honesty in academic work, and to the disciplinary guidelines and procedures applicable to breaches of such policy and regulations. Details may be found at https://registry.cuhk.edu.cn/en/page/30.

With each assignment, students will be required to submit a signed declaration that they are aware of these policies, regulations, guidelines and procedures. For group projects, all students of the same group should be asked to sign on the declaration. Only the final version of the assignment should be submitted via VeriGuide.
