Midterm Project Overview

The mid-term project is now underway. You may work individually or form a team.

Midterm Project Requirements

The Mid-Term Project focuses on problem identification, literature or repository analysis, and feasibility planning for a research or optimization proposal. Students may choose one of the following two tracks:

  • Track A (Research-Oriented): Literature review based on one core paper + at least three related works.
  • Track B (Application-Oriented): Analyze one LLM-related GitHub repo and propose an optimization (no code submission required). Reference at least three additional repos.

Deliverables: 15-min presentation (+5-min Q&A; recorded accepted) + concise PDF report via Blackboard.

Evaluation: Clarity of problem statement, feasibility of approach, depth of critical analysis.

Final Project Overview

The final project is now underway. You may work individually or in a group.

Final Project Requirements

The Final Project builds directly on the mid-term outcomes, requiring students to execute their research or implementation plan and complete empirical or implementation-based validation. Students must propose a clear research question (which may emerge from their literature review) and apply appropriate tools or methods to investigate and answer it effectively.

Presentation: 8-min talk + 2-min Q&A on May 6 (strictly enforced). All students must participate. Slides (PPT/PDF) due on Blackboard by end of day.

Submission: PDF final report + ZIP with code and data via Blackboard by May 10, end of day. Document individual contributions.

Evaluation: Contribution, Methodology, Results and Analysis, Presentation Quality, Code Reproducibility.
